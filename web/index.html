Your site passes every test and still looks broken.

Functional tests check that buttons click. They never see a headline shoved past the fold, two sentences printed on the same pixels, or a font that quietly fell back to Arial. A real browser does. So does eyeball.

Renders at 1440×900 and 390×844. No sign-up. Public URLs only.

What a real browser sees that a test runner doesn't

  • Past the fold. A headline taller than the first screen.
  • Text on text. A reveal or label pinned over the headline.
  • Wrong font. The brand face failed to load and rendered as Arial/system.
  • On the headline. A control or element sitting on the words.
  • Console errors. The page throws at load.
  • Side-scroll. Something wider than the viewport.

eyeball is the hosted version of an open-source render gate you can also drop into CI. Same engine.
